# VLANs

## What is a VLAN?

- VLANs (Virtual Local Area Networks) segment a physical network into multiple logical networks.
- They isolate traffic between groups of devices even on the same switch.

## Cybersecurity Relevance

- VLANs provide network segmentation, reducing the scope of attacks.
- They separate user, guest, server, and management traffic.
- VLAN misconfigurations can cause security gaps or allow unauthorized access.

## Usefulness in Cybersecurity

- VLANs support least-privilege network design and policy enforcement.
- They help contain breaches by isolating compromised hosts.
- VLAN-aware firewalls and ACLs can enforce strict inter-segment controls.

## Best Practices

- Use separate VLANs for sensitive systems and untrusted devices.
- Avoid VLAN hopping vulnerabilities by securing trunk ports and using proper switch configurations.
- Document VLAN assignments and verify traffic flows with security policies.
