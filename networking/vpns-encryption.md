# VPNs & Encryption

## What is a VPN?

- A VPN (Virtual Private Network) creates an encrypted tunnel over a public network.
- It protects data in transit and enables secure remote access.

## Encryption

- Encryption scrambles data so only authorized parties can read it.
- Strong protocols include AES, ChaCha20, and TLS.

## Cybersecurity Relevance

- VPNs secure remote connections and protect sensitive traffic from interception.
- Encryption is fundamental for confidentiality and integrity.
- VPNs are used for secure management, remote work, and site-to-site connectivity.

## Usefulness in Cybersecurity

- VPNs reduce the risk of man-in-the-middle attacks on public networks.
- Encryption protects authentication, web traffic, email, and file transfers.
- They are vital for regulatory compliance and protecting sensitive information.

## Best Practices

- Use strong VPN protocols (IKEv2, OpenVPN, WireGuard) and enforce multi-factor authentication.
- Keep encryption keys and certificates secure.
- Monitor VPN usage and ensure traffic is inspected by security tools when needed.
