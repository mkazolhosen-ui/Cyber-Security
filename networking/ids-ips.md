# IDS/IPS

## What are IDS and IPS?

- IDS (Intrusion Detection System) monitors traffic and alerts on suspicious activity.
- IPS (Intrusion Prevention System) can block or prevent malicious traffic in real time.

## IDS vs IPS

- IDS is passive and generates alerts.
- IPS is active and can enforce security actions.
- Both can use signature, anomaly, and behavior-based detection.

## Cybersecurity Relevance

- IDS/IPS are essential for detecting network-based attacks and policy violations.
- They help identify malware, scanning, exploitation attempts, and data exfiltration.
- IPS can stop threats before they reach critical assets.

## Usefulness in Cybersecurity

- IDS provides visibility and incident detection across the network.
- IPS helps automate response and prevent attacks from spreading.
- Combined with firewalls, IDS/IPS support comprehensive threat defense.

## Best Practices

- Tune detection rules to reduce false positives.
- Maintain up-to-date signatures and behavioral models.
- Place IDS/IPS sensors strategically near critical segments and network boundaries.
